Timeline of events (From the beginning of the pandemic to today)



  1. March 18, 2020
    1. Maryland legislature adjourns early due to COVID, detention bill dies
  1. March 29-April 5, 2020

 .               First “car caravan” protests held at Howard County Detention Center

  1. April 13, 2020

 .               State COVID-19 immigrant rights activist demand letter published 

a.              https://wearecasa.org/maryland-covid-19-demand-letter/ 

  1. April 30, 2020

 .               CAIR coalition receives preliminary injunction victory, secures release of one person at Howard County Detention Center due to staff member testing positive for COVID-19

  1. Summer, 2020

 .               Widespread protests against police brutality begin in May, including in Howard County

a.              Activist groups organize demos and indicate solidarity with Black lives

  1. June 4, 2020

 .               Broad DMV immigrant rights activist solidarity statement with BLM published

a.              https://www.afsc.org/document/black-lives-matter-solidarity-letter-immigrant-justice-movement 

  1. June 22, 2020

 .               Third demonstration held at the Howard County Detention Center against ICE and the contract

  1. July 19, 2020

 .               Massive demo held in Ellicott City against ICE and the detention center contract

  1. July 27, 2020

 .               Immigrant rights activists issue a call for a special state session on CJ reform

a.              https://wearecasa.org/maryland-covid-19-demand-letter/ 

  1. August 3, 2020

 .               Exec. Ball announces HoCo will join amicus brief against DHS’ policy of delaying asylum seekers work authorization

a.              Activists call on him to “start work at home” https://wearecasa.org/howard-county-executive-ball-claiming-support-immigrant-community/ 

  1. August 27, 2020

 .               CM Walsh introduces CB 51, a bill that end the IGSA. Activist groups including JUFJ, HoCo for Immigrant Justice, ACLU-MD are supportive of the bill.

a.              Walsh and other news outlets cited the protests as reason for the bill’s introduction

  1. September 18, 2020

 .               Exec. Ball announces executive order to “deeply sever” HoCo’s relationship with ICE but sustain the IGSA - in opposition to Councilmember Walsh’s bill

a.              CASA announces support for the exec order

  1. October 6, 2020

 .               CB 51 passes; CM’s Liz Walsh, Deb Jung, and Christiana Rigby vote yes

a.              Vetoed by Exec Ball

b.              HoCo Imm Justice Condemnation of veto: https://scotteblog.com/2020/10/10/howard-county-coalition-for-immigrant-justice-response-to-cb51-veto/ 

  1. November 7, 2020

 .               Joe Biden wins the election, immigrant rights activists (including CASA) respond

  1. January 5, 2021

 .               The 2021 MGA session begins

  1. February 10, 2021

 .               The first hearing for the reintroduced Dignity Not Detention Act takes place over Zoom in the Maryland State Senate Judicial Proceedings Committee (I attended).

  1. The Whole Month of March into April

 .               Various “Twitter storms” are conducted by CASA, JUFJ, and other partners in support of DND and the Trust Act. Usually using the hashtags #MDGA21 , #DignityTrust and #DignityNotDetention among others

  1. March 3rd, 2021

 .               First house hearing for DND occurs in the Judiciary Committee (I also attended)

  1. March 11th-12th, 2021

 .               Dignity Not Detention is reported favorably out of subcommittee and the Judiciary Committee in the house along party lines (I attended both)

  1. March 18th, 2021

 .               Exactly one year after the legislature adjourned prematurely, Dignity Not Detention passes the House of Delegates with a sweeping majority (I attended both third reader and the bill vote)

  1. March 22nd, 2021

 .               Howard County Executive Calvin Ball reverses course and announces the county will be ceasing their detention contract with ICE within 60 days. 

  1. Between March 22nd-March 31st

 .               Behind closed doors, Maryland state lawmakers in the Senate decided to push for an amalgamated Dignity Not Detention Act that would include the prohibitive portions of the MD Trust Act, but would not deal with existing 287(g) programs (DND was untouched). Reportedly this was due to the decreased likelihood of the Senate passing two immigration bills in one session.
